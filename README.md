The project downloaded and stored all (n = 611 as of 30/07/2025) Domestic Homicide Reviews (DHRs) from the government website: https://homicide-review.homeoffice.gov.uk/

From breaking the DHRs into text, the process resulted in the 12.2million words contained in the DHRs being refined to 52,304 unique tokens, which were then analysed to understand how animals presented across the DHRs. 

The Wakefield package was then used to get a comprehensive list of animal names, which was used to identify every occurrence of animals across the 611 DHRs. 

This resulted in an extract of 1625 instances an animal was mentioned, which was manually reviewed by the research team.
